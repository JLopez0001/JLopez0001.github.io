Introduction

Meet Calm Space, our innovative electronic medical records (EMR) system designed to streamline the healthcare experience. Calm Space offers a dynamic dual-user platform that allows therapists and QA specialists to manage patient profiles, diagnoses, and notes with unprecedented ease and efficiency.

With enhanced UI transparency and a robust Mongoose-based database, every team member stays informed and empowered, ensuring better healthcare outcomes and seamless documentation workflows.

Idea behind Calm Space

I created Calm Space out of a need I saw firsthand as a quality assurance specialist working with a EMR system. The existing system's interface was confusing for providers, causing providers to overlook critical alerts, like note rejections, which could delay patient care for days. UI Experience

Getting Started

First the user will need to sign in with their unique username and password. If they do not have an account then they will need to register.

Therapist home page

Once the therapist signs in they will be greeted with their patients caseload. The patient card will hold the patient basic demographic informaiton as well as a link to the patients chart.

Creating a patient

In order to create a patient I wanted the UI to be as simple as possible with just the basic demographic information of the patient needed.

Patient Chart

When the therapist wants to either add a note or diagnosis they will be able to see all of the patients notes/status as well as appointment times and type of service.

Creating a note

In order for a therapist to sign a note and send it to QA for review they will need to input what occured in the session as well as what goals were tackled.

Method of service is cruicial to determine how the session was conducted.

Lastly in order to sign the note, the therapist must re-input the patient unique id, the QA provider code, and their own provider ID.

QA task page

Upon submission of the note, it will go to the QA task box to determine if the note needs to be rejected or approved.

Reviewing a note

Depending if the note passes the criteria needed based on the Department of Mental Health. The QA specialist can approve the note or reject it.

If the note is rejected the QA specialist will need to provide a reason for the rejection and then the note will be sent back to the therapist to make corrections.

Challenges

Major Challenge on this Project

The main challenge I encountered on this project was the feedback aspect of sending rejected notes back to the therapist that wrote the note. Along side that, having the status change upon if the note was rejected,pending, or approved. But luckily with my experience in QA I had the general idea on how to go about tackling this project. It was also great to have a visual representation of a working EMR system so I can reference.

Models

Since I used MongoDB and Mongoose for my database, I underestimated the difficulty of determining what should go in the schema and how many models I need. I determined the best approach was to create 3 models, notes, patients, and users.
