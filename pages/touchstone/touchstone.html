Introduction

TouchStone offers a unique and engaging way to learn Braille. Through the combined use of AI assistance and interactive quizzes, the app creates an enhanced experience that guides users through the basics of Braille, covering the representations of simple numbers, the alphabet, special characters and everyday words that are likely to be encountered.

With the integration of Gemini AI in TouchStoneâ€™s search bar, you can easily inquire about the Braille translation of any word or letter. Gemini AI will provide a detailed text explanation and an image of the Braille translation, making learning both comprehensive and visually accessible.

Idea behind TouchStone

The inspiration for TouchStone lies behind a shared passion for education and accessibility, alongside our interests in language and communication. Our motivation to create a learning app for Braille stemmed from a memory shared by our peer as they reminisced about their childhood best friend, who happened to be visually impaired. Despite their differences, they formed an inseparable bond exchanging handwritten notes in Braille by poking holes through pieces of paper. The friend created an entire cheat sheet of Braille representations that they knew in order to preserve that precious connection.

Home Page

The purpose of this app is to make Braille learning as easy as possible. So, we wanted this app to focus on the basics of Braille.

Alphabet Card

With every Alphabet card we decided to have an image associated with the letter that is being stored in the backend database.

Quiz Directory

The best way to learn is to test yourself. So, we added 3 quiz features where the user can select anyone of them and enhance their learning.

Matching Quiz

Here's an example of the quiz feature. With matching the user will see 6 random cards and they will have to match the Braille that is associated with it.

Search Feature

With the help of Gemini A.I. The user is able to request any translation for a specific letter or word and the request will be sent to the backend where Gemini will provide a response with an associated Braille translation image.

Challenges

Major Challenge on this Project

We started the project with very limited knowledge in A.I. So, we had to teach ourselves as we moved forward while trying to make progress in a 2 week time frame that we had to complete this project.

Researching Braille

Finding reliable and consistance information about Braille proved more difficult than anticipated. Different websites use different binary codes to signify Braille such as Braille binary or ASCII code so we needed to dive deep into each one and see what would fit our project better.
